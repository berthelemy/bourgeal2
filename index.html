nestled in brittany

a place to share in the Breton countryside

character and comfort

Arranged over three floors, the farmhouse offers accommodation for up to 8 people, with a mix of old French charm and modern facilities.

Dating from the mid-18th Century, the house retains many original features, and is set in a small hamlet with views over the open countryside.

plenty to discover

Whether your preference is to explore the Breton countryside on foot or by bike; to have surfing adventures at the coast; to discover the rich history of the region; or simply to relax and enjoy the Breton way of life, you'll find that the house at Bourgeal provides a great base for these activities.

a beautiful setting

The farmhouse is located in the hamlet of Bourgeal, just 3 kilometres from the beautiful market town of Le Faouet.

Surrounded by fields and woodlands, you can take a walk down quiet country lanes straight from the back door, with barely a vehicle to disturb you.

the house

traditional features combined with simplicity and comfort

an old granite farmhouse

The house is constructed with thick granite walls and oak beams.

The farmhouse kitchen on the ground floor is accessed from the front of the house.

The living room, bathroom and master bedroom are all on the first floor. Access is either from an internal wooden staircase situated in the kitchen, or from an external flight of stone steps at the rear of the house.

Further sleeping accommodation is found in the second floor grenier.

the living room

The first floor living room is an inviting space, with its original beams, huge granite fireplace and wood-burning stove, and comfortable seating.

You'll find this a welcoming space to relax or to enjoy the selection of board games, books and maps at the end of a busy day.

There's an ample supply of logs and kindling in the barn

the farmhouse kitchen

As you enter the kitchen at the front of the house through the stable door, you'll notice a flight of steps on your right which lead up to the rest of the house.

On your left is a comfortable seating area, and a wood-burning range (warm, fun and easy to cook on!).

Straight ahead you'll find a large farmhouse table and chairs, electric cooker, fridge (with ice-box) and washing machine.

outdoor space

the immediate vicinty and outdoor living

Outside, there's a small front garden to the front of the house, and a larger garden with barn and garage at the back.

The house is situated by a quiet country lane, making it perfect for post-dinner evening strolls with superb views of the surrounding countryside.

It's great to eat outdoors at every opportunity. A rustic picnic table and benches in the back garden, and small bistro table and chairs at the front make eating 'en plein air' a real pleasure.

see & do

beautiful countryside, stunning beaches, fascinating heritage

lovely country walks

From the front door you can walk down a quiet country lane with views of rolling countryside off into the distance. Depending on the season, why not forage for blackberries, hazelnuts or sweet chestnuts along the way?

With its network of footpaths and greenways, Morbihan has plenty of options for those who enjoy exploring the countryside at a gentler pace. At the house you’ll find books and maps to help you plan your day. We also have two bikes for people to use.

inviting beaches

With the nearest beach only 45 minutes drive away, it’s easy to spend the day by the sea. The coast of Brittany has so many beautiful resorts and isolated coves to discover - whether you enjoy swimming, sun-bathing, or surfing, you'll soon find your favourite beach!

so many places to discover...

Some of our favourite places include:

  • the art gallery/museum in Le Faouët
  • the Chapelle Sainte-Barbe
  • the Abbaye Saint-Maurice set on the banks of the Laïta river
  • the Roche du Diable
  • Pont-Aven
  • Concarneau
  • the submarine museum in Lorient

location

Bourgeal is located in countryside towards the south of Brittany.

It is approximately 2hrs 20 mins from the ferry port of Saint-Malo (for ferries from Portsmouth), and 1hr 30 mins from Roscoff (for ferries from Plymouth). There are also ferries which run from Portsmouth to Caen, Cherbourg and Le Havre, and one which runs from Poole to Cherbourg.

If you choose to drive from Calais (having taken the channel tunnel), it will take you about 7 hrs.

If you prefer to fly, Ryanair and Flybe provide services to Dinard and Brest.

There is a driveway at the house with space for two vehicles.

shops

On the way in to Le Faouët from Bourgeal there's an excellent boulangerie for all your bread and pâtisserie needs. Le Faouët also has a range of other shops all gathered around the impressive C16th market hall.

There are supermarchés at Le Faouët (beware lunchtime closing!) and Scaër, and a hypermarché north of Le Faouët at Gourin.

local markets

The following are just a selection of the many markets which happen throughout the week in Brittany:

  • Le Faouët - 3rd Wednesday mornings
  • Scaër - Saturday mornings
  • Rosporden - Thursday mornings
  • Gourin - Monday mornings

tourist information

There is an excellent tourist information office in Le Faouët, just off the market square.
