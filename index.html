the house

Simplicity and comfort are words which best describe the house at Bourgeal. An uncluttered look allows the character of this traditional Breton farmhouse to shine through.

Arranged over three floors, the house offers accommodation for 6 people with a mix of old French charm and modern facilities.

Key features of the house include impressive wooden beams throughout and a floor-to-ceiling granite fireplace in the first floor living room.

A wood burning cooker takes pride of place in the farmhouse kitchen, and the room is further equipped with an electric cooker and hob, washing machine, fridge/freezer and an electric steamer.

And no French kitchen would be complete without a corkscrew!

Wooden stairs indoors, and granite steps at the back of the house, lead up to the first floor living room, master bedroom and bathroom with shower.

With its well-cushioned settees, two comfortable chairs and wood burning stove, the living room provides a welcoming space to relax or to enjoy the selection of board games, books and maps at the end of a busy day.

Further stairs lead up to the grenier - a massive loft space with a vaulted ceiling and exposed beams. Here you will find two twin bedrooms at one end, and a ‘chill-out’ space at the other (with an added sofa bed for further sleeping accommodation).

Outside, there is a small garden to the front of the house, and a larger garden with barn and garage at the back of the house. A rustic picnic table and benches make eating ‘en plein air’ a real pleasure.

see & do

Bourgeal is a peaceful hamlet in the département of Morbihan, southern Brittany. From the front door you can walk down a quiet country lane with views of rolling countryside off into the distance. Depending on the season, why not forage for blackberries, hazelnuts or sweet chestnuts along the way?

With its network of footpaths and greenways, Morbihan has plenty of options for those who enjoy exploring the countryside at a gentler pace. At the house you’ll find books and maps to help you plan your day. We also have two bikes for people to use.

Or maybe you prefer to head for the coast? With the nearest beach only 45 minutes drive away, it’s easy to spend the day by the sea. The south coast of Brittany has so many beautiful resorts and isolated coves to discover, and being on the Atlantic, the waves are good!

Some other places well worth exploring include:

  • the art gallery/museum in Le Faouet
  • the Chapelle de Sainte Barbe
  • the Abbaye de Saint-Maurice set on the banks of the Laita river
  • the Roche du Diable
  • Pont Aven
  • Concarneau
  • the submarine museum in Lorient

Food-wise, there are many regional specialities to discover and enjoy. Whilst Brittany isn’t a wine growing region, it certainly is a cider region! Being coastal, seafood features highly on most menus, and it’s impossible to travel through a town without spotting a handful of creperies, serving sweet crepes and savoury galettes.

Location

Bourgeal is located in countryside towards the south of Brittany.

It is approximately 2hrs 20 mins from the ferry port of St Malo (for ferries from Portsmouth), and 1hr 30 mins from Roscoff (for ferries from Plymouth). There are also ferries which run from Portsmouth to Caen, Cherbourg and Le Havre, and one which runs from Poole to Cherbourg.

If you choose to drive from Calais (having taken the channel tunnel), it will take you about 7 hrs.

If you prefer to fly, Ryanair and Flybe provide services to Dinard and Brest.

There is a driveway at the house with space for two vehicles.
